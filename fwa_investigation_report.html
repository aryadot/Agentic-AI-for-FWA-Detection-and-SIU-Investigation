Wound Allograft Overbilling
Billing for medically unnecessary services / diagnosis-procedure mismatch

Provider Information

Provider: Coastal Dermatology Associates
Specialty: Dermatology
Location: Miami, FL
Prior Investigation: False

Core Anomaly Identified

The claim for procedure code Q4121 (Amniotic wound allograft application) on 2026-05-15 for $14,500.00 with diagnosis code S00.01XA (Superficial abrasion of scalp, initial encounter) significantly exceeds peer averages and lacks prior authorization.

Investigation Reasoning Chain

Step 1 Diagnosis code supports procedure
Finding: False
Significance: The diagnosis code S00.01XA (Superficial abrasion of scalp, initial encounter) does not clinically support the application of an amniotic wound allograft (Q4121) as superficial abrasions are minor and typically heal without such interventions.
Step 2 Billing volume and peer comparison
Finding: The provider is in the 99th percentile for billing compared to peers in the same specialty and geography, with a 200% deviation above the peer average and a 280% increase in volume since 2026-02-01.
Significance: This indicates a significant deviation from expected billing patterns, suggesting potential overbilling.
Step 3 Prior authorization
Finding: Prior authorization was required but not obtained.
Significance: Lack of prior authorization raises concerns about the legitimacy of the billing process.

Alternative Explanations Considered

Explanation considered: Unusual patient condition
Assessment: While possible, there is no evidence provided that the patient's condition was unusually severe or complex to justify the use of an allograft for a superficial abrasion.
Explanation considered: Billing error or coding mistake
Assessment: The significant deviation from peer averages and the specific lack of clinical support for the procedure suggest that this is more than a simple error.
Explanation considered: Educational or training purposes
Assessment: There is no indication that the procedure was part of an educational or training program, and such activities would typically have clear documentation and oversight.

Key Evidence

  • Diagnosis code S00.01XA does not support the medical necessity of procedure Q4121.
  • Significant deviation from peer billing averages (200% above) and volume spike (280% increase).
  • Lack of prior authorization for the procedure.
HIGH SUSPICION
HIGH SUSPICION — Escalate to Full Investigation
The combination of a significant billing deviation, lack of clinical support for the procedure, and absence of prior authorization strongly suggests potential fraud or abuse.

Recommended Action for SIU Analyst

The SIU analyst should request additional documentation from Coastal Dermatology Associates, including medical records supporting the necessity of the allograft application for the patient's condition, and investigate further to determine if there was a legitimate reason for the deviation from standard billing practices.
